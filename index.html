Multimodal Laughter Detection

Stack : Python, Hubert, Marlin

This project was designed to create a system that could accurately detect and classify laughter by analyzing both audio and video data. The goal was to improve how machines understand human emotions and behaviors in multimedia content. To achieve this, the system integrated two models: Hubert for analyzing audio signals and Marlin for processing video data. By employing a technique called late fusion, which combines the results from both audio and video analysis, the system was able to significantly enhance its overall accuracy. While analyzing audio alone gave an accuracy of 40.37% and video alone achieved 83.85%, the fusion of both modalities resulted in a much higher accuracy of 87.08%. The impact of this project lies in its ability to improve the way machines interpret non-verbal communication, such as laughter. This has potential applications in areas like entertainment, virtual assistants, and human-computer interaction, where understanding emotional cues can enhance user experiences.

Potato Leaf Disease Detection

This project involved developing a mobile application using Flutter to help detect abnormalities in plants or leaves at different stages of growth. The app was designed to assist farmers or gardeners in identifying potential diseases early, preventing crop damage and improving plant health. A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model was built to analyze images of plants and accurately detect signs of disease. The model was trained on various stages of plant growth, enabling it to recognize abnormalities with an accuracy of 94.9%. The impact of this project lies in its practical application for agriculture. By providing an easy-to-use tool for early disease detection, the app can help users make timely decisions to treat plants, reducing crop losses and improving yields. This kind of technology can be especially useful for improving efficiency in farming practices.
